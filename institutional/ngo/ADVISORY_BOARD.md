## Overview

The Advisory Board provides expert guidance, strategic counsel, and credibility to the MeetBarter Foundation without formal governance authority.

---

## Purpose

The Advisory Board serves to:

- Provide specialized expertise in key domains
- Offer strategic guidance to Board and management
- Enhance Foundation credibility and reputation
- Facilitate partnerships and networking
- Advocate for the Foundation's mission

**Note:** Advisors have **no voting rights** and **no fiduciary responsibility**. They serve in a consultative capacity only.

---

## Advisory Board Members & Roles

### 1. **Economic Development Advisor**

**Expertise Area:** Alternative economics, barter systems, local currencies

**Primary Responsibilities:**

- Advise on economic model sustainability
- Provide insights on barter market dynamics
- Review pricing and escrow mechanisms

- Quarterly economic model reviews
- Research on barter economics

## Advisory Board Meetings

**Frequency:** Bi-annual (twice per year) + ad-hoc consultations as needed

**Format:** Virtual or in-person strategic sessions

**Agenda:** Set by Board Chairperson and Executive Director

**Participation:** Advisors may attend by invitation; no quorum required

---

## Compensation

**Honorarium:** Small annual stipend or per-meeting fee (optional)

**Expenses:** Reasonable travel and meeting expenses reimbursed

**Recognition:** Public acknowledgment on website and materials

**Equity:** No equity or ownership stake

---

## Term & Commitment

**Term:** 2-year renewable terms (no limit on renewals)

**Time Commitment:** 10-20 hours per year (flexible)

**Meetings:** 2 formal meetings + occasional consultations

**Exit:** Advisors may resign at any time with 30 days notice

---

## Selection Criteria

Advisors are selected based on:

- Relevant expertise and credentials
- Alignment with Foundation mission and values
- Diversity of perspectives and backgrounds
- Availability and willingness to contribute
- Network and connections that benefit Foundation

---

## Code of Conduct

Advisors must:

- Maintain confidentiality of sensitive information
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Provide honest and constructive feedback
- Respect the Board's decision-making authority
- Uphold the Foundation's reputation and values
