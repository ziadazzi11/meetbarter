## Statutes of the Association

### Article 1: Name and Headquarters

The name of the association is **"Meetbarter Lebanon"** (hereinafter referred to as the "Association").
The headquarters is located at:
**Zouk Mosbeh, Street 73, St. Antoine Building, 5th Floor, Lebanon.**

### Article 2: Objectives

The Association is a scientific and social non-profit entity aimed at:

1. Developing and managing a secure digital barter ecosystem to support local commerce.
2. Establishing a "Humanitarian Escrow" (Social Shield) to provide community aid during economic crises.
3. Promoting ethical trade practices and digital literacy in the field of resource exchange.
4. Implementing forensic audit standards for transparency in social grants.

### Article 3: Membership

Membership is open to all individuals and entities who share the Association's mission and adhere to its technical and ethical standards. Categories include:

- **Founding Members**: The initial steering committee.
- **Active Members**: Regular participants in the ecosystem.
- **Ambassadors**: Verified community leaders overseeing local sectors.

### Article 4: The General Assembly

The General Assembly is the supreme authority of the Association, composed of all registered active members. It shall meet annually to review reports and elect the Board of Directors.

### Article 5: The Board of Directors

The Association is managed by a Board of Directors composed of at least five members (CTO, COO, Legal, Community, and Moderation leads). The Board oversees day-to-day operations and technical custody.

### Article 6: Financial Resources

The Association's resources consist of:

1. Membership fees and donations.
2. Administrative allocations from the Meetbarter Protocol (the 10% Logistics Fee).
3. Grants from local and international philanthropic bodies.

### Article 8: Ethical Prohibition (Weaponry, Military, & Controlled Substances)

1. The Association strictly prohibits the exchange of:
    - Firearms, ammunition, or military-grade equipment.
    - Weaponized robotics or tactical combat systems.
    - Narcotics, pharmaceutical drugs, and natural psychedelics (e.g., mushrooms).
    - Medical supplies and equipment requiring governmental authorization or professional permits.
    - **Human Trafficking & Exploitation**: Selling persons, forced labor, or passport retention.
    - **Human Organs & Tissue**: Trading of any body parts, fluids, or surrogacy services.
    - **Harmful Occult Rituals**: Items related to "satanic" rituals, black magic, or any materials promoting spiritual harm.
2. Any attempt to circumvent this prohibition will result in an immediate item block and automatic account suspension upon repeat offenses (Insistence Rule).
3. This policy is non-negotiable and serves to protect the humanitarian integrity and legal standing of the Association.

### Article 9: Institutional Business Onboarding & Liability Shield

1. **Protocol**: Legitimate businesses may apply for "Institutional Partner" status by submitting valid commercial registers, tax IDs, and professional permits.
2. **Verification**: The Association's administration shall verify the legitimacy of all submitted documents. Verification must be renewed every 12 months.
3. **Badge**: Approved businesses will receive the "Institutional Partner" badge, signaling high-fidelity trust to the community.
4. **Absolute Prohibition**: Institutional Partner status **never** waives the prohibitions outlined in Article 8. The exchange of firearms, ammunition, military gadgets, and weaponry remains strictly forbidden for all users, regardless of verification tier or external commercial permits.
5. **Liability**: The Association serves as a verification infrastructure. While we verify institutional legitimacy, the Association is not liable for individual commercial disputes between partners, provided the Verification Protocol was followed.
